About Synap Step

A hardware invention.
Guided by a smart app.

Synap Step is a health-tech concept centered around a wearable hardware device — the core invention — supported by a companion mobile app that serves as the prototype interface for testing, personalization, and demonstration.

Right now, we are starting with the app. It uses your phone's built-in haptic engine to simulate the feedback the hardware will eventually deliver. What starts as your phone's vibration will evolve into a precision wearable with purpose-built hardware that delivers targeted haptic therapy exactly where Parkinson's patients need it most.

The app is intentionally built with a simple, clean, and accessible UI so that anyone can pick it up and use it immediately. The three core features are AI driven and sensor powered — the system monitors, learns, and responds automatically.

Note: The app is the prototype interface. The hardware is the product. These sessions are not traditional exercise — they are short, coordinated motor-based neurological activities designed to sustain mobility and coordination.

Tremor Reduction

AI and sensory feedback monitor tremor patterns in real time and automatically deliver the right haptic response to calm hand movement — no manual input needed.

Neurological Exercise Section

Fully customizable guided motor sessions. Users adjust type, duration, and frequency of short neurological movement activities designed to keep motor pathways active.

Haptic Feedback During Walking

AI and sensory monitoring detect movement in real time and automatically deliver directional haptic cues to help users stay balanced and navigate with confidence.

AI Adaptability

The system learns each individual user over time — tracking subtle tremor shifts, movement vectors, and motor responses to continuously refine its feedback.

Our Why

Built for the people who need it most.

There is no cure for Parkinson's disease. That is just the reality right now, and honestly it is part of why we started building this in the first place. Krish Madhavan, one of our leads, had a close relative who suffered from Parkinsons but sadly lost their life due to external medical conditions. Watching someone you love struggle to pick up a glass of water or eat a meal on their own is not something you forget. An app like Synap Step could have helped his grandfather do those things. That is not a small thing to us.

Every small step Synap Step helps someone take is a step toward something bigger. Consistent neurological engagement does not just help the individual — it contributes to a broader reduction in the secondary consequences of Parkinson's and age-related decline, including fall-related injuries, emergency hospitalizations, and the rapid deterioration that often follows a loss of mobility.

We also want to keep this affordable. The people who need Synap Step most should not be priced out of it. The app stays free. The hardware will be priced around 40 to 45 dollars. Because an app that only reaches wealthy users is not really solving the problem we set out to solve.

The Problem

Why existing solutions fall short.

01

Tremors Disrupt Daily Life

Parkinson's tremors involve a massive number of variables — amplitude, rhythm, directional vectors, muscle fatigue, medication cycles, and emotional state — all changing constantly throughout the day.

02

Hardware Exists But Is Inaccessible

Devices using haptic or electrical stimulation for tremors are confined to research settings. They are bulky, not ergonomically designed for daily wear, and priced completely out of reach for most families.

03

No All-in-One App Solution

No consumer app today addresses tremor reduction, neurological movement guidance, and directional walking support together in one simple platform. These problems have always been treated separately.

04

Decline Without Engagement

Regular neurological engagement can help preserve motor pathways. But existing tools are reactive and impersonal — there is a critical gap between clinical therapy and accessible daily support.

How We Can Solve It

Intelligent support,
tailored to each person.

Synap Step combines haptic feedback technology, AI-driven personalization, and short motor-based neurological movement guidance into a single accessible system. The hardware detects tremor frequency and responds with precision counter-stimulation. The app learns from every session and continuously refines the guidance it provides.

Our hardware concept draws inspiration from the adaptive haptic feedback technology in the PlayStation 5 controller — precision actuators capable of simulating highly specific resistance and frequency patterns — repurposed into a small wearable bracelet or ring form factor.

What We Are Building

Every component built with purpose.

Wearable Haptic Device

The core invention — a small bracelet or ring form factor with precision haptic actuators designed specifically for therapeutic tremor stabilization and motor support.

Tremor Frequency Detection

Onboard sensors lock onto each user's tremor signature, reading amplitude, directional vectors, rhythm, and muscle response simultaneously to enable real-time adaptive feedback.

Neurological Movement Sessions

Short science-backed motor activities — not traditional exercise — that stimulate neurological pathways responsible for coordination and motor control, fully customizable per user.

Companion App Interface

Currently using the phone's haptic engine as a proof of concept. Handles personalization, session scheduling, device calibration, and user data with a simple clean UI.

AI and Machine Learning

Adaptive algorithms learn from every session — adjusting haptic profiles, movement difficulty, and reminder timing based on how each individual responds day to day.

Elder-Friendly Design

Every interface decision prioritizes simplicity and clarity. Built so that someone who has never used a health tech app before can pick it up and understand it immediately.

A note on our movement guidance: The sessions Synap Step delivers are not workouts or physical therapy exercises. They are intentional, coordinated motor-based neurological activities designed to stimulate the pathways associated with motor control and help sustain movement capability over time.
